The headline

This part of the account runs lean on paper: 7.41x combined ROAS over 30 days, €1.14M in 60-day revenue, and a TACoS of about 4.7% if we project monthly. The numbers look healthy because the catalog is huge and a handful of auto-style mega-campaigns carry most of the weight. Underneath: 51 SP campaigns and 17 SB campaigns, four of the SP campaigns have 12 ad groups each mixing 1,500+ ASINs apiece, Top of Search converting at 19.93x ROAS but receiving only 0.8% of impressions, Sponsored Brands underperforming at 3.74x ROAS, Sponsored Display effectively dormant at €834 / 30d, and €10K of identifiable waste sitting in the search-term report. The account isn't bleeding dramatically — it's under-managed.

The €10,025 negation opportunity 64d

Across the Search Term Report covering the last 64 days, 455 individual search terms each received 50+ clicks and produced zero orders. Combined wasted spend: €10,025 — roughly €4,700 per month of recoverable budget that's sitting in plain sight.

Why 50+ clicks is the threshold

This part of the account converts at an average 0.90% CVR. A search term that's received 50+ clicks should statistically have produced at least half an order. Zero orders after 50 clicks means the term isn't unlucky — the listing doesn't match shopper intent for that query. 50+ clicks is the threshold that survives scrutiny in any negation review.

Sponsored Brands underperforms its potential 30d

The numbers

17 client SB campaigns spending €2,252 over 30 days, returning €8,430 — a 3.74x ROAS that's well below the 5–7x range typical for branded headline ads in this category. Three of the campaigns produce zero sales. The rest are concentrated in three ad groups: Balkonsichtschutz Blatt (€575), Ersatz Bezug Generic (€530), Schließfach Generic (€362) — all of which suggest the structure was set up but not maintained.

The opportunity here isn't to add more SB campaigns — it's to fix the ones already running. SB Headline ads at 3.74x ROAS in a category where 6x is normal means the keyword targeting or creative is off. A proper restructure (better keyword segmentation, video SB layer, brand defense isolation) would lift this to 5x+ on the same budget.

Sponsored Display is essentially dormant 30d

The numbers

€834 total SD spend over 30 days across 16 client campaigns (most paused or with zero activity). Combined SD sales: €3,515. The ROAS isn't the issue — it's healthy at 4.21x — the issue is volume. SD should represent 15–25% of total PPC spend on an account this size; here it's 3%.

Most SD campaigns are named generically ("Garten Display", "SD_Pergolen_PAT_..."), most are paused, and the one campaign generating real sales (Mülltonnenverkleidung PAT) does it with €103 spend and €1,571 in sales — proof that SD does work in this category when actually run.

Pull the top 50 competitor ASINs by 64d sales attribution. Build dedicated PAT campaigns per top-revenue product against those ASINs with deliberate bids. The 5,388 invisible ASINs 60d

The math

7,428 in-scope ASINs are live. 2,040 have at least one unit sold in the 60-day Business Report. 5,388 ASINs — 72.5% of the active catalog — produced zero revenue in two months. Some are seasonal or discontinued, but a meaningful portion are simply ranked too deep in search results to be found.

Without concentrated TOS pressure on target keywords for 2–4 weeks per new listing, products either rank organically by accident or never rank at all. If even 5% of these invisible ASINs could be recovered through ranking PPC, that's 269 additional products contributing to topline.

The question Helium 10 keyword tracking would answer definitively: what are the top keywords for those invisible ASINs and where are they ranking organically? Without that data we can only note the gap. The hypothesis is straightforward: in competitive categories like Garten and Haushalt, organic ranking without PPC pressure is rare.

How these numbers were built

Adds TOS placement modifiers on the 42 fixed-bid SP campaigns to push more budget into 19.93x ROAS placement (modeled at +3-5% sales lift). Restructures SB from 3.74x to 5x (adds ~€2.8K monthly from same SB spend).

90–120 day scenario: Restructures the five mega-campaigns into per-ASIN STAG (Single Theme Ad Group) structure (5–8% efficiency gain). Scales SD remarketing from €834 to €3-4K monthly at current 4.21x ROAS. Builds deliberate PAT competitor campaigns at 9–10x ROAS (modeled at +€10K monthly). Adds 10–15 ranking campaigns on highest-potential invisible ASINs. Excludes any organic ranking lift from sustained TOS visibility.

Caveats: projections over 30 days are partly model and partly faith. The forecast assumes competent execution — a half-done restructure could hurt before it helps. M63 (high-priced daybed, mentioned earlier) and similar premium-priced products won't scale through PPC alone — they need listing/pricing work first.

Bottom Line

  • The account is profitable but under-managed. 7.41x combined ROAS over 30 days looks healthy because a handful of auto-style mega-campaigns do most of the work — not because anyone is actively optimizing.
  • €10,025 of waste is identifiable in the 64d Search Term Report (~€4,700/mo recoverable). 455 terms with 50+ clicks and zero orders. Lowest-risk intervention available.
  • Top of Search converts at 19.93x ROAS but receives 0.8% of impressions. Adding TOS placement modifiers on the 42 fixed-bid campaigns is the single highest-leverage change available.
  • Sponsored Brands underperforms (3.74x vs 5-6x benchmark) and Sponsored Display is dormant (€834/30d). Both layers are set up but not actively managed; restructure adds 30-50% more sales from same spend.
  • Competitor ASIN targeting already generates €88K of PPC sales at 11.49x ROAS over 64 days — by accident. Formalizing this into deliberate PAT campaigns is the biggest unclaimed opportunity in the account.
  • Realistic 90–120 day upside: +€78K monthly PPC sales through negation, TOS optimization, mega-campaign restructure, formal PAT, SD scale-up, SB restructure, and ranking campaigns on invisible ASINs. Most of the lift comes from work the account's own data already proves works.
