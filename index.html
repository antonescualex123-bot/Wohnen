The headline

This part of the account runs lean - 2.07% TACoS and 7.87x ROAS. The numbers look healthy because the catalog is huge and a handful of auto-style mega-campaigns carry most of the weight. Underneath that surface: 51 campaigns covering 7,428 ASINs, four of them with 12 ad groups each mixing hundreds of products, 71.5% of search-term spend producing zero orders, Top of Search receiving 0.8% of impressions despite converting at 16.64x ROAS, and Sponsored Display essentially absent. The account isn't bleeding dramatically - it's under-managed.

The €10,083 negation opportunity

Across the client-managed search-term report, 457 individual terms each received 50+ clicks over 60 days and produced zero orders. Negating them recovers €10,083 - about 18% of the client's search-term budget - with no creative work, no listing changes, and no risk.

Why 50+ clicks is the threshold

The client account converts at an average 0.90% CVR. A search term that's received 50+ clicks should statistically have produced at least half an order. Zero orders after 50 clicks means the term isn't unlucky - the listing doesn't match shopper intent for that query. A lower threshold (€5+ spend, which maps to roughly 30 clicks at current CPC) would flag €13,348 across 893 terms, but some of those are noise. 50+ clicks is the number that survives scrutiny in any negation review.

The bigger picture is worse than the recoverable number suggests: 71.5% of all search-term spend in this part of the account (€40,790 of €57,049) produced zero orders. Most of that is dust - terms that spent €1-4 and didn't convert - but the pattern is unambiguous: nobody is actively negating.

These five campaigns are doing a lot of heavy lifting. A15 alone generates €17K in 60 days. They're profitable - that's not the problem. The problem is visibility and control:

  • 535 ASINs in a single ad group (A15-K30) means per-ASIN bid optimization is impossible. One bid covers 535 products at different prices, margins, and competitive positions.
  • No per-product copy because ad copy is attached to the product listing, but grouped management means winners and losers get the same budget treatment.
  • Negative keyword hygiene is broken. Negating a term inside a 535-ASIN ad group risks killing a converter that happens to match on a different ASIN.
  • Harvesting is manual and slow. When these campaigns discover a winning search term, promoting it to a proper exact-match campaign requires finding which of 535 ASINs it belongs to.

Sponsored Display is practically absent

The numbers

€970 total SD spend in 60 days across 18 campaigns (13 active). Combined 60d SD sales: €3,515. For context: this is an account with €1.14M in 60-day revenue, 2,073 ASINs with sales, and an obvious opportunity in every category where shoppers compare 5-10 products before buying. SD should be 15-25% of total PPC spend - here it's 4%.

Beyond the volume gap, the SD setup itself shows that nobody is actively managing it. Of 18 campaigns, most are named generically ("Garten Display", "SD_Mendler_Pergolen_PAT_...") and several are paused with zero activity. There's no systematic view-remarketing, no competitor ASIN defensive layer, no category-based audience targeting.

34% of client PPC spend is accidentally hitting competitor ASINs through substitutes targeting, producing 48% of all client PPC sales at a ROAS nearly 50% above account average. This is happening by accident. Every other category of PPC activity would get more attention if the agency running it knew how much of the revenue came from this specific mechanic.

The play

Pull the top 50 competitor ASINs by 60d sales attribution. Build dedicated PAT campaigns per top-revenue product against those ASINs with deliberate bids. Use "Targeting Expansion" in PAT to pick up lookalike competitor listings. Shift part of the Auto-campaign budget into these PAT campaigns once they prove out.

The risk is minimal because the data proves the demand. The payoff is structural - turning a happy accident into a system means it can be scaled, optimized, and measured properly.

The ranking gap deserves emphasis. Out of 51 client-managed campaigns, not one has a "ranking" or "launch" purpose. With 7,428 active ASINs and only 2,073 generating sales, 5,355 ASINs produce zero revenue. Some of those are seasonal or discontinued, but many are simply ranked too deep in search results to be found. Without concentrated TOS pressure on target keywords for 2-4 weeks per new listing, products either rank organically by accident or never rank at all.

The 5,355 invisible ASINs

The math

7,428 client-managed ASINs are live. 2,073 have at least one unit sold in 60 days. 5,355 ASINs - 72% of the active catalog - produced zero revenue in two months. If even 5% of those could be recovered through ranking PPC, that's 268 additional ASINs contributing to topline.

This is the question Helium 10 data would answer definitively - what are the top keywords for those invisible ASINs and where are they ranking organically? Without that data, we can only note the gap. The hypothesis is straightforward: in competitive categories like Garten and Wohnen, organic ranking without PPC pressure is rare. Anything not actively pushed is invisible.

How these numbers were built

30-day scenario: Conservative, low-risk. Removes €10,083/60d wasted spend (terms with 50+ clicks and zero orders) with no compensating loss - the negated terms produced nothing anyway. Total spend drops, sales hold flat, TACoS drops to ~1.2%.

60-day scenario: Reinvests saved €10,083 at current client ROAS of 7.87x = ~€79K extra sales over 60d (€39.6K/mo), then discounted ~50% because reinvestment never holds the original ROAS and new keywords degrade. Adds TOS placement modifiers on the 42 fixed-bid campaigns to capture more of the 16.64x ROAS placement - modeled at +5-8% account-wide sales lift.

90-120 day scenario: Restructures the five mega-campaigns into per-ASIN STAG (Single Theme Ad Group) structure (5-8% efficiency gain). Adds proper SD remarketing at €3-4K/mo at 4-5x ROAS. Builds deliberate PAT competitor campaigns around the already-winning competitor ASINs at 9-10x ROAS. Adds 10-15 ranking campaigns on the highest-potential invisible ASINs. Excludes any organic ranking lift from sustained TOS visibility (historically adds 5-10% topline over 8-12 weeks but not guaranteed).

Caveats: projections over 30 days are partly model and partly faith. The forecast assumes competent execution of all changes - a half-done restructure could hurt before it helps.

Bottom Line

  • 91.7% of the account is under-managed, not broken. 7.87x ROAS and 2.07% TACoS look healthy because a handful of auto-style mega-campaigns do most of the work - not because anyone is actively optimizing.
  • €10,083 is recoverable in 30 days through negation alone. 457 terms with 50+ clicks and zero orders - at 0.90% average CVR they should have produced at least half an order each, but didn't. Lowest-risk intervention in the deck.
  • Top of Search converts at 16.64x ROAS but receives 0.8% of impressions. Adding TOS placement modifiers on fixed-bid campaigns is the single highest-leverage change available.
  • Competitor ASIN targeting already generates €89K of client PPC sales at 11.30x ROAS - by accident. Formalizing this into deliberate PAT campaigns is the biggest unclaimed opportunity in the account.
  • Realistic 90-120 day upside: +€51K monthly PPC sales through negation, TOS optimization, mega-campaign restructure, formal PAT, SD layer, and ranking campaigns on invisible ASINs. Most of the lift comes from work the account's own data already proves works.
